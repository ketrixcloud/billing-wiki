To connect your domain to CloudFlare, you first need to buy it.

## Where to buy a domain

We recommend the following places to buy a domain:

- **CloudFlare** — yes, you can buy a domain right there.
- **Network Solutions** — we use their services ourselves, a great platform.
- **REG.RU** — if your domain is `.ru` or `.su`; not supported in the Ukrainian zone.

You can buy a domain elsewhere too, but there's usually a markup, so we recommend these places specifically.

## Connecting the domain

Once you've bought the domain, move on to connecting it.

If the domain was bought on CloudFlare, there's nothing extra to configure — it was added automatically when you bought it.

If it was bought somewhere else, you need to set up **nameserver (NS) records**. An NS record is a special record that connects your domain to a different provider.

> Example (very abstract, but the same idea applies): you bought your own coffee shop, but it's located in the city of Mariupol — you need special records so it's counted as being in a different city.

### 1. Click the Add domain button

![The Add domain button on the CloudFlare domains page](how-to-connect-cloudflare-domain/add-domain-button.png)

### 2. Select a plan

Yes, CloudFlare is a paid platform, but it has a free tier. We recommend starting with that one.

![Selecting the free plan](how-to-connect-cloudflare-domain/select-free-plan.png)

### 3. Click Continue to activation

Without changing anything in the suggested DNS records, click the continue button.

![The Continue to activation button](how-to-connect-cloudflare-domain/continue-to-activation.png)

### 4. Copy the nameservers and add them at your registrar

This is the most important step. You need to copy the nameservers CloudFlare gives you — there are 2 of them — and add them at your domain registrar.

We can't show exactly how to change nameservers at your registrar — it's different for every one. After the change, you should be left with **only the 2** nameservers CloudFlare gave you — for example, like in the screenshot.

![Example of the CloudFlare nameservers to keep and the old registrar nameservers to remove](how-to-connect-cloudflare-domain/cloudflare-ns-records.png)

### 5. Wait 5-10 minutes and click I updated my nameservers

After changing the nameservers, wait **5-10 minutes** (important — not less, the records usually don't have time to update sooner). Then click **I updated my nameservers**.

![The I updated my nameservers button](how-to-connect-cloudflare-domain/updated-nameservers-button.png)

### 6. Done!

Go back to your domains tab (where you started this guide). If the domain's status is **Active**, congratulations, you've done everything right.

## What to do if it shows Invalid nameservers?

![The Invalid nameservers status in the domains tab](how-to-connect-cloudflare-domain/invalid-nameservers-status.png)

If it shows **Invalid nameservers** — in 90% of cases that's normal. Your registrar (especially if it's not a European one) may take longer than 5-10 minutes to update the nameservers, and it can take up to **24 hours**.

Don't rush — check back in 5-6 hours. If it still shows **Invalid nameservers**, you can contact our support so we can check whether everything was done correctly. Usually, it should work within 5-6 hours.

In rare cases the process can take the full 24 hours — it depends on the registrar, especially if the domain is a free one.
