Joining our team is, in principle, possible.

> **Important!** We are not hiring support agents.

Why is that? We've automated as much as possible, and because of that we get very few support tickets.

For the people who still have questions, we have trained support agents who know **3 languages** and understand the technical side of the project.

## Why we don't hire new support agents

1. There's a hard requirement — knowing 3 languages: **English, Russian, Ukrainian**.
2. Understanding everything that's going on in the project.

As a rule, the only people who become support agents are those we invite ourselves — because we see they've been with us for a long time already (either as a client or a partner) and have picked up enough experience along the way.

Hiring new people off the street just isn't rational. To bring someone new onto the team, we'd have to train them on every process and on our way of communicating with clients. That's a lot more work than it sounds.

### Ask yourself

Think through the following questions in your head:

- What is a chargeback initiated by the cardholder's issuing bank?
- What's the difference between x32 and x64 architecture, and why does building old games run into an architecture-selection problem 90% of the time? How would you help someone fix it?
- Why did the slime chunks on a Minecraft server disappear entirely? Where exactly is the problem? Could you analyze the modpack/build and find it?
- Why are you willing to put up with rude or disrespectful language directed at you? Answer that honestly — you're a person with dignity.
- What is nginx, what is Apache, what is web proxying? Why do we offer a free IPv6 for a VDS, but not "any port you want" for free on any server, even if that port is unused?

...and a whole range of similar questions.

99% of the time, our people know the details of all of it — from Minecraft, to building websites and connecting domains, to fixing Linux bugs like a hypervisor error.

## Why it's worth joining

Working with us is experience you won't find anywhere else. Here you learn to think outside the box, to look for gaps and holes in a process, and to keep asking yourself "why this way, and not another" — that's exactly what we teach.

It's a great opportunity to gain experience.
